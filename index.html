The Old Man And the Sea

Book's Summary

The Old Man and the Sea is about an old fisherman, Santiago, who struggles to catch a giant marlin after 84 days without a catch. The story deeply symbolizes life’s struggles where the sea represents life itself, fishing stands for hard work and survival, and the marlin is a noble goal worth fighting for. Though Santiago returns with only the fish’s bones, his endurance, bravery, and silent dignity earn him deep respect. He fails in result, but wins in spirit. showing that true heroism lies in fighting with purpose, even when the world sees you as defeated.

Key Themes

  • Perseverance in the face of struggle.
  • The dignity of hard work.
  • The relationship between humans and nature.
